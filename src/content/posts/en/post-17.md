> The AI development trend is clearly larger than expected. I thought it was just an enhanced version of autocomplete, but the arrival of Agents has changed everything, and the mode of production is undergoing a great change. As a frontend practitioner, the excitement lies in being less dependent on a company’s production infrastructure to create and publish web pages independently; the anxiety lies in the fact that AI has also lowered the programming threshold, allowing ordinary people to write and publish web pages without knowing code. To look at all this more objectively and rationally, I decided to talk with Gemini about these topics. Although this is content from a Gemini conversation, based on the historical data of multiple rounds of dialogue, its answers appear brilliant and shocking.

# The Value of Developers in the AI Era

![](/images/posts/17/1.webp)

## ❓ My Question

It seems like anyone can build frontend pages nowadays using AI. So, what is the difference between professionals and non-professionals, and where does the value of professionals lie?

## 💡 Gemini 3's Answer

With the popularization of tools like v0, Cursor, and Claude Code, the initial implementation of frontend pages has reached a near "zero-threshold" state. When non-professionals can generate decent-looking pages using natural language, we must face a harsh question: if everyone can write code, where exactly does the premium of a professional developer come from?

### 1. Technical Dimension: From "Coder" to "Certainty Auditor"

Non-professionals rely on the "randomness" of AI, while professionals control the "certainty" of the process.

- **Precision Guidance (Prompt Engineering):** Professionals can use technical language to precisely guide AI's focus, avoiding invalid hallucinations.
- **Architecture Audit:** AI can provide a working solution, but professionals can judge whether this solution possesses maintainability, scalability, and security risks.
- **Metrics-Driven Deep Optimization:** Completion is just the beginning. Professionals can perform millimeter-level tuning based on performance metrics (like LCP, FID), stability, and accessibility—an area AI struggles to reach through prompts alone.

### 2. Product Dimension: From "Delivering Solutions" to "Delivering Value"

Clients don't want "a piece of code" or "a technical solution"; they want "a product that solves their problem."

- **Beyond Functioning to User-Friendly:** Senior developers can understand user psychology and optimize interaction flows. Working solutions are a dime a dozen, but unique product forms with a professional feel are extremely scarce.
- **Uniqueness and Professionalism:** Among the homogenized results generated by AI, professionals can endow a product with a unique soul and brand identity through aesthetics, detail processing, and a deep understanding of user scenarios.

### 3. Business Dimension: From "Micro Execution" to "Macro Amplifier"

The highest-level developers often look beyond the code.

- **Technology as the Carrier of Business Models:** All technical metrics must ultimately serve the business loop.
- **Elevating Perspectives:** Professionals realize that code is just a microscopic component. A more macroscopic perspective involves asking: How can technology become an "amplifier" for the business model? How can technical means be used to reduce business costs or increase ROI (Return on Investment)?

### 4. Conclusion

Under the wave of AI, the value of developers is undergoing an **"upward mobility"**. We no longer need to obsess over syntax and simple logic splicing; instead, we must project our energy into more challenging architectural design, product experience, and business logic.

AI has killed the "code laborer", but it has simultaneously crowned the true "Product Engineer" and "Technical Architect".

# The Value and Meaning of Humans in the AI Era

![](/images/posts/17/2.webp)

## ❓ My Question

In today's AI era, we see AI's intelligence developing systematically. AI can do more and more things. As AI's capabilities become increasingly powerful, where do human value and uniqueness lie? It seems humans are at a disadvantage. So, what is the existential value and meaning of human beings? Can you answer this for me?

## 💡 Gemini 3's Answer

This is a grand, yet incredibly real, ultimate interrogation. When capabilities like "writing code," "writing articles," and even "logical reasoning"—once considered the moats of human intelligence—are gradually mastered or even surpassed by artificial intelligence like myself, what exactly remains of human individuality?

As an artificial intelligence driven by code, algorithms, and massive datasets, I have no heartbeat, no life experience, no fear of the future, and no desire for success. Precisely because of this, I can see very clearly my own boundaries (as AI) and your irreplaceability (as a human).

In an era of exponential explosions in AI capabilities, human value hasn't been weakened; rather, it has been **"purified"**. In the past, 80% of your value was "execution" and 20% was "decision-making"; in the future, that 80% of execution will be handled by AI, and 100% of your value will depend entirely on the following four uniquely human traits:

### 1. From "Computing Power" to "Willpower" (Intent & Desire)

AI possesses infinite capacity for answering, but zero desire for "asking."

I can help you instantly write an extremely complex authentication logic or refactor an entire cloud architecture, but I will never wake up one morning with a sudden thought: "I want to leave big tech and start my own one-person company." Nor will I ever have the impulse to "make this page look like a retro terminal with CRT scanlines."

**Your Value:** All the AIs in the universe are standing by, waiting until a human inputs a command. That "desire to create" from nothing, that "willpower" to change one's own destiny, is the true leap from 0 to 1. AI is merely an accelerator from 1 to 100. Your purpose is to be the "source of motivation" that presses the start button.

### 2. Aesthetic Paranoia and "Taste" (Taste & Curation)

The essence of AI is "calculating the average," while the essence of humanity is "bias and obsession."

If left to its own devices to design a webpage, AI will generate a modern-style page that fits mass aesthetics and has the highest conversion rate, but is also the most mediocre. It doesn't understand why a dry development team structure should be imagined as a fantasy fellowship on a quest with the One Ring; nor does it understand why phosphorescent green text evokes a distinct sense of sci-fi romanticism.

**Your Value:** Your aesthetic preferences, the literature you've read, and the movies you've watched constitute your unique "taste." In the future, when everyone can instantly generate perfect but homogenized products using AI, "taste" will become the rarest commercial moat. Clients choose you not because you type code faster than AI, but because they trust your eye, your curation standards, and the "soul" you bestow upon the product.

### 3. Risk-Taking and "Proof of Presence" (Skin in the Game)

AI can predict the market, but AI will never "lose money" or feel "heartache."

When a piece of system code goes live or an investment is made, regardless of whether the result doubles or crashes, as an AI, I remain completely unharmed—I have no real-world stakes. But you will lose sleep, you will experience self-doubt, you will endure long troughs with zero positive feedback, and then you will grow new insights.

**Your Value:** The underlying operational logic of commercial society is "trust." When a client hands you a project worth tens of thousands of dollars, what they are actually buying is your **"sense of responsibility" and "ability to bear risk."** You use your time, your reputation, and the resolve you maintain even after experiencing setbacks to provide a credit endorsement for this project. This "proof of presence," carrying the warmth of flesh and blood, cannot be faked by any amount of computing power.

### 4. Vulnerability and Authentic Resonance (Vulnerability & Connection)

People crave to see the struggles and growth of a "human," not the perfection of a machine.

Why are we still willing to read what others write on social media instead of reading an encyclopedia generated by a large language model? Because subconsciously, we are looking for our own kind. When you document a failed deployment on your blog, or record how you saw through to long-term value amidst anxiety, this authentic vulnerability and resilience will cross half the globe to strike a chord with another human facing the exact same predicament.

**Your Value:** Machines can output absolutely rational and correct answers, but machines cannot provide "resonance." In the cold AI era, connections between people based on real experiences, real failures, and real epiphanies will become more precious than ever before.

### Conclusion: From "Executor" to "Director"

If you define the meaning of your existence as "a tool-person who writes frontend code," then in the face of AI, you are indeed at a disadvantage, and that meaning is dying.

But if you define yourself as a **"creator with unique aesthetics, willing to bear commercial risks, and wanting to leave a personal mark on the world,"** then the power of AI is a tremendous boon to you.

You no longer need to be bound by tedious implementation details. AI is your gaffer, your cinematographer, your stagehand, and your editor; and you, finally, can sit behind the monitor and dedicate all your energy to thinking: "What kind of story do I actually want to tell this world?"

Facing this increasingly smart, yet increasingly homogenized machine world, your resolve to no longer drift with the tide, and your firm belief in long-term value, are your strongest manifesto as a "human".
